# Aftertale — Value Proposition

> The anchor for the addon's brand and UX. When a design decision is
> ambiguous, it should resolve in favor of the three things below.

## The core tension

Aftertale is **deliberately silent**. It records your playthrough as moments in
the background and never interrupts you. That silence is the feature — but it is
also the threat. A player can't value what they can't perceive.

## The problem

A new player installs the addon, opens it once, and sees a minimap popover full
of insider vocabulary with no explanation of **what it does, why it's running,
or where the payoff lives**. Because the chronicle can only be *read* on
aftertale.gg — and the addon historically never said so — nothing on-screen
connects the silent recording to a tangible reward.

## The risk

Without a clear, in-addon value prop, the install-to-uninstall window is
brutally short. The player perceives a quiet, cryptic UI doing nothing visible
and removes it before the website ever proves the point.

## What we're solving for

The addon itself — not just the website — must, within seconds of first open,
make a new player understand:

1. **What it's doing** — silently recording the meaningful moments of *this*
   character's journey.
2. **That it's working** — visible, plain-language proof (the stat sheet) that
   real moments are being captured *right now*.
3. **Where the payoff is** — that these moments become a written chronicle,
   readable at aftertale.gg.

## Success looks like

A player who installs Aftertale gets it immediately, sees evidence it's already
capturing their story, understands the reward is one click away — and leaves it
installed long enough for the website to deliver.

## Implications for design

- **Plain language over mystique.** Self-explanatory stats beat evocative
  jargon ("beats," "vigil"). Retire vocabulary that needs a glossary.
- **Name aftertale.gg as a feature**, not a CTA. The watch records here; the
  chronicle is read there. Saying so is the whole value loop.
- **Show, don't just tell.** Live counts of captured moments are proof the
  silent recorder is working.
- **Quiet, not invisible.** Notifications should stay out of the way (on-brand
  chat lines, not intrusive popups) while still confirming the addon is alive.
